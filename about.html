About EEOC Explained

We make complex workplace rights and discrimination laws understandable for everyone — employees, HR, and advocates.

Our Mission

Provide clear, accurate, and practical explanations of U.S. employment discrimination laws and the EEOC complaint process so people can make informed decisions with confidence.

What We Do

  • Break down the EEOC process step-by-step in plain language.
  • Curate official links, forms, and trusted guides.
  • Offer checklists and prep tips for filing or responding to a charge.
  • Promote fairness, access, and self-advocacy at work.

What We Don’t Do

  • No legal advice. EEOC Explained is an independent educational resource and is not a law firm.
  • No representation. We don’t represent individuals or act on your behalf with the EEOC or employers.
  • No affiliation. We are not affiliated with the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission.

If you need legal advice, consider consulting an employment attorney or your local bar association. See our Resources for directories.

Disclaimer

Content on this site is for educational purposes only and may not reflect the most current legal developments. Always verify with official sources such as EEOC.gov or a licensed attorney in your jurisdiction.

Accessibility

We aim for clear language, keyboard-friendly navigation, and readable contrast. If you encounter a barrier, please let us know so we can improve.

Privacy (Short)

We don’t sell personal data. Pages may use basic, privacy-respecting analytics to improve the site. Do not submit sensitive details through forms; use the official EEOC Public Portal for filings.
