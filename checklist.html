Step-by-Step Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare and file an EEOC charge of discrimination, or to understand next steps if you’ve already filed.

1) Confirm coverage & deadlines

  • Employer size: Most discrimination laws apply if the employer has 15+ employees (20+ for age discrimination). Some state/local laws cover smaller employers.
  • Deadline: You usually must act within 180 days of the discriminatory act (often up to 300 days if a state or local law also applies).
How to File a Charge (EEOC) Find Your EEOC Office

2) Gather facts & documents

  • Timeline of what happened (dates, places, people involved).
  • Job info (title, department, supervisor, employment dates).
  • Evidence (emails, texts, write-ups, performance reviews, photos, policies).
  • Names/contact info for witnesses.

Tip: Keep originals safe. Upload or copy only what you need.

3) Decide how to start

You can begin online, by phone, or in person:

Start Online (EEOC Public Portal) Call EEOC: 1-800-669-4000 Visit a Field Office

TTY: 1-800-669-6820 · ASL Video Phone: 1-844-234-5122 · 711 (relay)

4) Intake interview & charge drafting

  • Complete the online intake questionnaire or speak with an EEOC staffer.
  • Review your draft charge carefully before signing and submitting.
Log in to EEOC Portal

5) After filing: what to expect

  • Mediation: Many cases are offered free, voluntary mediation early on.
  • Investigation: If not resolved, EEOC may investigate (documents, interviews, position statements).
  • Notice of Rights: If the EEOC does not find cause or cannot continue, you’ll receive a “right-to-sue” notice with a deadline.
EEOC Mediation Filing Overview

6) Keep records & follow deadlines

  • Track all portal messages and mail. Note every due date.
  • Keep copies of anything you submit or receive.

Need help?

We’re an independent educational site. For official case updates or filings, use the EEOC Public Portal. For general questions, you can call the EEOC directly.
